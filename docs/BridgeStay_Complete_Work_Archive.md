## Executive Summary

BridgeStay is a PropTech platform designed to solve the housing crisis facing international students at the University of Utah and across the Salt Lake City metropolitan area. Unlike traditional rental platforms such as Zillow and KSL Homes that require Social Security Numbers and U.S. credit histories, BridgeStay enables international students to secure verified housing using only their passport and I-20 documentation. The platform combines bilingual support (English/Chinese), verified landlord listings, and AI-powered roommate matching to create a trusted marketplace that bridges the gap between international students and local property owners.

With over 3,165 international students currently enrolled at the University of Utah alone—representing a 15% increase in first-time international students—and Salt Lake County ranking among the most expensive housing markets in the nation, BridgeStay addresses a critical unmet need in a rapidly growing market segment. [1] [2]

---

## 1. Problem Statement

### The International Student Housing Crisis

International students face a unique set of challenges when seeking housing in the United States that domestic students simply do not encounter. These barriers create significant stress, financial burden, and in some cases, prevent talented students from pursuing their educational goals altogether.

**Documentation Barriers.** The most fundamental challenge is the requirement for a Social Security Number (SSN) and U.S. credit history. According to Zillow's own help documentation, applicants without an SSN cannot receive a background check, making them effectively ineligible for most rental applications. [3] Similarly, KSL Homes requires a $50 tenant screening fee that includes credit and background checks—services that are impossible to complete for students who have never lived or worked in the United States. [4]

**Remote Booking Anxiety.** International students must often secure housing before arriving in the United States, creating a perfect environment for scams and exploitation. Without the ability to physically visit properties, students rely on online listings that may be fraudulent, outdated, or misrepresented. Industry research confirms that "unfamiliarity with neighborhoods and market norms exposes students to scams, especially when searching online before arrival." [5]

**Financial Vulnerability.** International students face compounding financial pressures: higher out-of-state tuition rates, currency exchange fluctuations, limited work authorization, and housing costs in markets like Salt Lake City that rank among the most expensive in the nation. The average studio apartment near the University of Utah now costs $1,467 per month, representing a 6% annual increase. [2] [6]

**Language and Cultural Barriers.** Even students proficient in English struggle with legal terminology in rental agreements, cultural expectations around roommate etiquette, and communication with landlords unfamiliar with international student needs. Chinese students, who represent the largest international student demographic, often rely on informal WeChat groups that lack verification or consumer protections.

| Challenge | Traditional Platforms | BridgeStay Solution |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| SSN Requirement | Required for screening | Passport/I-20 verification |
| Credit History | Required (600+ score) | Not required |
| Language Support | English only | Bilingual (EN/CN) |
| Scam Protection | Limited verification | Admin-verified listings |
| Remote Booking | No guarantees | Verified photos and landlords |

## 3. Competitive Analysis

### Current Market Landscape

The Salt Lake City rental market is dominated by three primary channels, each with significant limitations for international students.

**Zillow** represents the largest online rental marketplace nationally, with over 4,380 listings in Salt Lake City. However, Zillow's tenant screening process requires an SSN for background checks. Their official documentation states: "You can still submit an application without a Social Security number. However, without an SSN, CIC is unable to provide a background check." [3] This effectively disqualifies international students from most Zillow-listed properties.

**KSL Homes** is Utah's dominant local classifieds platform, deeply integrated with local media and trusted by Utah residents. Their tenant screening process requires a $50 fee for credit and background checks that depend on U.S. financial history. [4] While KSL offers extensive local inventory, their platform provides no specialized support for international applicants.

**CSSA WeChat Groups** (Chinese Students and Scholars Association) serve as informal housing networks within the Chinese international student community. While these groups offer Chinese-language communication and peer recommendations, they lack any verification system, creating significant scam risk. Listings are informal, disputes have no resolution mechanism, and the platform excludes non-Chinese international students.

### BridgeStay's Competitive Advantages

| Feature | Zillow | KSL Homes | WeChat Groups | BridgeStay |
|---------|--------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| No SSN Required | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Verified Listings | Partial | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Bilingual Support | ❌ | ❌ | Chinese only | EN/CN |
| Roommate Matching | ❌ | ❌ | Informal | AI-powered |
| Scam Protection | Limited | ❌ | ❌ | Admin-verified |
| UofU Focus | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

BridgeStay's differentiation centers on three core advantages: **verification** (all listings reviewed by administrators), **accessibility** (no SSN or credit history required), and **localization** (bilingual interface with UofU-specific expertise).

## 4. Business Model

### Revenue Streams

BridgeStay employs a multi-stream revenue model designed to scale with platform growth while maintaining accessibility for students.

**Primary Revenue: Listing Commission (10% of First Month's Rent)**

When a student successfully signs a lease through BridgeStay, the platform collects a commission equal to 10% of the first month's rent from the landlord. At an average rent of $1,400/month near UofU, this generates $140 per successful placement. This model aligns incentives: landlords pay only for results, and students access the platform free of charge.

**Secondary Revenue: Premium Landlord Services**

Landlords can purchase enhanced visibility through featured listings ($29/week, $79/month, $199/quarter), priority placement in search results, and professional photography services. These premium tiers increase exposure to the platform's targeted international student audience.

**Tertiary Revenue: Value-Added Student Services**

As the platform scales, additional revenue streams include roommate matching services (premium AI-powered compatibility matching), furniture rental partnerships, airport pickup coordination, and move-in assistance packages. These services address the complete international student relocation journey.

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Estimated at $25 per student through targeted campus marketing and WeChat community engagement.

**Lifetime Value (LTV):** $140 initial commission plus potential for repeat bookings during 4-year academic career and referrals to incoming students. Estimated LTV: $300+.

**LTV:CAC Ratio:** 12:1, indicating strong unit economics and sustainable growth potential.

## 5. Sales & Marketing Strategy

### Campus-Centric Go-to-Market Approach

BridgeStay's marketing strategy leverages the concentrated nature of the international student community and the institutional infrastructure of the University of Utah.

**Phase 1: Lassonde Entrepreneur Institute Partnership**

As a Lassonde-affiliated venture, BridgeStay will establish presence within the Lassonde Studios community, home to over 400 student entrepreneurs. Activities include:

- Pop-up information sessions during international student orientation weeks
- Integration with Lassonde's mentor network for landlord recruitment
- Showcase at Lassonde Demo Day events to build credibility and awareness

**Phase 2: International Student & Scholar Services (ISSS) Collaboration**

The University of Utah's ISSS office provides critical support services to all 3,165 international students. BridgeStay will pursue formal partnership to:

- Include BridgeStay in official housing resource guides distributed to admitted students
- Present at ISSS orientation sessions and "info sessions" mentioned in university communications [1]
- Provide housing workshops addressing visa-compliant lease terms

**Phase 3: Chinese Student Community Engagement**

Chinese students represent the largest international demographic at most U.S. universities. BridgeStay's bilingual platform and WeChat integration enable direct engagement through:

- Partnership with UofU Chinese Students and Scholars Association (CSSA)
- Sponsored content in Chinese student WeChat groups
- Ambassador program recruiting current Chinese students as brand advocates

**Phase 4: Digital Marketing & Content Strategy**

- SEO-optimized content targeting searches like "UofU international student housing" and "Salt Lake City apartments no SSN"
- Xiaohongshu (Little Red Book) presence for Chinese student discovery
- YouTube/Bilibili video guides on securing housing in Utah

// Animated counter component
function AnimatedCounter({ target, suffix = "", prefix = "" }: { target: number; suffix?: string; prefix?: string }) {
  const [count, setCount] = useState(0);
  const ref = useRef(null);
  const isInView = useInView(ref, { once: true });

  useEffect(() => {
    if (isInView) {
      const duration = 2000;
      const steps = 60;
      const increment = target / steps;
      let current = 0;
      const timer = setInterval(() => {
        current += increment;
        if (current >= target) {
          setCount(target);
          clearInterval(timer);
        } else {
          setCount(Math.floor(current));
        }
      }, duration / steps);
      return () => clearInterval(timer);
    }
  }, [isInView, target]);

  return (
    <span ref={ref}>
      {prefix}{count.toLocaleString()}{suffix}
    </span>
  );
}

export async function fetchSupabaseListings(): Promise<SupabaseListing[]> {

    const { data, error } = await supabase
      .from('listings')
      .select('*')
      .order('luxury_score', { ascending: false });

    if (error) {
      // Properly format the error for logging
      const errorMessage = error.message || JSON.stringify(error);
      console.error('Error fetching listings from Supabase:', errorMessage);
      // Return empty array instead of throwing - graceful degradation
      return [];
    }

    return data || [];
  } catch (err) {
    // Handle network errors or other exceptions
    const errorMessage = err instanceof Error ? err.message : 'Unknown error';
    console.error('Supabase connection error:', errorMessage);
    return [];
  }
}

// Delete a listing from Supabase by ID
export async function deleteSupabaseListing(id: string): Promise<boolean> {
  try {
    const { error } = await supabase
      .from('listings')
      .delete()
      .eq('id', id);

    if (error) {
      const errorMessage = error.message || JSON.stringify(error);
      console.error('Error deleting listing from Supabase:', errorMessage);
      return false;
    }

    return true;
  } catch (err) {
    const errorMessage = err instanceof Error ? err.message : 'Unknown error';
    console.error('Supabase delete error:', errorMessage);
    return false;
  }

    setIsLoadingSupabase(true);

      const data = await fetchSupabaseListings();
      const mapped = mapSupabaseListings(data);
      setSupabaseListings(mapped);

        (typeof error === 'object' ? JSON.stringify(error) : String(error));
      console.error('Failed to fetch Supabase listings:', errorMessage);
      // Keep existing listings on error (graceful degradation)
    } finally {
      setIsLoadingSupabase(false);
    }
  };

    refreshSupabase();
  }, []);

    localStorage.setItem(STORAGE_KEY, JSON.stringify(listings));
